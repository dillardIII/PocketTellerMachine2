In response to your request for a logic strategy note, here are several key strategies we could employ in our trading activities to optimize our performance and results:

1. **Deep Market Research**: To make informed decisions, comprehensive market research is crucial. The research should encompass both macroeconomic data and the specific financials about our interests. Understanding global economic trends and their implications on our trade offerings is the cornerstone of making robust trading decisions.

2. **Risk Management**: Every trading decision carries inherent risks. Establishing a robust risk management strategy will help mitigate these risks. This includes setting stop-loss and take-profit points, diversifying portfolio, and never risking more than a certain percentage of the portfolio on a single trade.

3. **Emotion Control**: Emotional trading can lead to poor decision making. Our strategy should incorporate discipline and patience. This means not succumbing to FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) or panic selling, but following the planned strategy and maintaining composure even in volatile market conditions.

4. **Adapting to Volatility**: Markets can fluctuate drastically. Our strategy should factor in this volatility and adapt accordingly. This could involve using trading tools like options and futures to hedge against potential loss.

5. **Frequent Review**: It's key to our success to constantly review and adjust our strategies based on market changes and our performance. Reviewing our past trades will give an idea about the effectiveness of decisions and indicate where adjustments can be made.

6. **Continuous Learning**: The trading industry is dynamic, thus, we need to stay informed about new trading practices, tools, and platforms. This could involve attending webinars, engaging in trading forums or camps and constantly reading up on industry trends.

7. **Use of Technology**: Leverage technology for analysis, prediction, and execution of trades. AI algorithms, for instance, can perform complex trades with high efficiency and accuracy.

Remember, there's no one-size-fits-all strategy in trading. Our approach should remain flexible and ready to adapt to the ever-changing market conditions.
